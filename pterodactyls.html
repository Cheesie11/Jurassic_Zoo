The Pterodactyls

Pterodactyls were a type of flying reptile that lived during the late Jurassic and early Cretaceous periods, approximately 150 to 100 million years ago. They were not actually dinosaurs, but rather belonged to a group of reptiles called pterosaurs. Pterodactyls were known for their distinctive wings, which were made of skin stretched between elongated fingers. They were some of the earliest vertebrates to evolve powered flight, and were able to soar through the skies in search of prey or to avoid predators.

facts

  • Type: Flying Reptile
  • Lifespan: 5-10 years
  • Weight: 20-50 kilograms
  • Speed: 50-60 km/h
  • Food: Meat
