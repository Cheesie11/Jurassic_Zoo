The Dodo

The dodo bird is an extinct species of flightless bird that once lived on the island of Mauritius in the Indian Ocean. It is known for its unique appearance, with a plump body, short wings, and a large, hooked beak. Despite being a ground-dwelling bird, the dodo was actually related to pigeons and doves. Unfortunately, the dodo became extinct in the late 17th century due to hunting by humans and the introduction of non-native species to the island. Although it is no longer with us, the dodo remains a fascinating and important symbol of the impact that humans can have on the natural world.

facts

  • Type: Bird
  • Lifespan: 20-30 years
  • Weight: 10-20 kilograms
  • Speed: 15 km/h
  • Food: Plants, Fruits, Seeds
